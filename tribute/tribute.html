Sr. Nelson Mandela

South African anti-apartheid activist and socialist

Here's a timeline of Nelson Mandela:

  • July 18, 1918 - Born in Mvezo, Eastern Cape
  • 1925 - Mandela begins primary school near his home village of Qunu. His teacher, Miss Mdingane, gives him the English name “Nelson”.
  • 1930 - Father dies. Entrusted to Thembu Regent Jongintaba Dalindyebo at the age of 12.
  • 1934 - Undergoes initiation; Attends Clarkebury Boarding Institute in Engcobo
  • 1937 - Attends Healdtown, the Wesleyan College at Fort Beaufort
  • 1939 - Enrolls at the University College of Fort Hare, in Alice
  • 1940 - Expelled
  • 1941 - Escapes an arranged marriage; becomes a mine security officer; starts articles at the law firm Witkin, Sidelsky & Eidelman.
  • 1942 - Completes BA through the University of South Africa (UNISA)
  • 1942 - Begins to attend African National Congress (ANC) meetings informally
  • 1943 - Graduates with BA from Fort Hare; Enrolls for an LLB at Wits University.
  • 1944 - Co-founds the ANC Youth League (ANCYL); marries Evelyn Ntoko Mase.
  • 1948 - Elected national secretary of the ANCYL.
  • 1956 - Arrested and later joins the others on trial for treason.
  • 1962 - Leaves the country for military training and to garner support for the ANC.
  • 1993 - Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize with President FW de Klerk.
  • 1994 - Becomes the president
  • 1995 - Establishes the Nelson Mandela Children's Fund.
  • 1999 - Steps down after one term as President, establishes the Nelson Mandela Foundation.
  • 2013 - Passes away at home in Johannesburg.
